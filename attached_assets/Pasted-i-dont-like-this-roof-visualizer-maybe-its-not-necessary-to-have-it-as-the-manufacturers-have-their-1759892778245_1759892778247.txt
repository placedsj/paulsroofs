## Our Comprehensive Winter-Ready Roof Inspection
Your First Line of Defense Against a New Brunswick Winter.

A small issue in October can become a major disaster by February. The cycle of heavy snow, ice buildup, and freeze-thaw temperatures is relentless on a roof. Our inspection is a top-to-bottom assessment designed to identify potential failures before they happen, saving you from costly emergency repairs and giving you peace of mind all winter long.

### What We Examine: The Paul's Roofing Checklist
Our team conducts a thorough, multi-point inspection, leaving no shingle unturned.

‚úÖ Shingle & Surface Analysis
We walk every accessible part of your roof to physically inspect the condition of your primary defense. We look for:

Cracked, curled, or missing shingles.

Significant granule loss (the sandpaper-like coating) on asphalt shingles.

Any loose or damaged metal panels and fasteners.

Signs of rot or soft spots on the roof deck.

** sealant inspection & Penetrations**
Over 90% of roof leaks occur at points where the roof is penetrated. This is our most critical checkpoint. We examine:

Flashing: The metal strips around chimneys, skylights, and vents. We check for rust, damage, or improper installation.

Sealants: The caulking and seals around pipes and vents. We look for cracking, peeling, or gaps that allow water intrusion.

Ventilation Caps: We ensure all roof vents are secure and undamaged.

üíß Gutter & Drainage Flow-Check
Clogged gutters are the primary cause of ice dams, which can force water under your shingles and into your home.

We inspect all gutters and downspouts for leaves, nests, and debris.

We ensure water can flow freely away from your foundation.

We check that gutters are securely attached to the fascia.

### What You Get: A Clear Path Forward
After the inspection, you're not left with a simple pass/fail. We provide you with a complete diagnostic package.

üì∏ Digital Photo Report
We don't just tell you, we show you. You'll receive a secure link to a gallery of high-resolution photos documenting our findings. You'll see exactly what we see, with clear notes on any areas of concern.

üìù No-BS Written Summary & Action Plan
You'll get a straightforward summary of our findings in plain English. We categorize any recommended work so you can make an informed decision:

Urgent Repairs (Priority 1): Issues that pose an immediate risk and should be addressed before the first snowfall (e.g., major leaks, significant flashing damage).

Preventative Maintenance (Priority 2): Items that are not yet critical but will likely become problems within the next year (e.g., resealing vents, replacing a handful of damaged shingles).

Good to Monitor (Priority 3): Areas showing normal signs of aging that we'll keep an eye on for you.
